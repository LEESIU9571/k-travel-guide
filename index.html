Getting Around Korea
Subway
Seoul subway from scratch (step-by-step):
  1. Buy a T-money card at a convenience store (CU/GS25) or at many subway station shops.
  2. Charge (top up) your T-money at a station machine: choose 충전 (top up), insert cash or use a card, and add enough for your trip.
  3. Plan your route in an app like Naver Map or KakaoMap, then note the line color, direction, and your destination station name.
  4. At the station, follow signs for the correct line. Go to the platform that matches your direction (the board will show your destination).
  5. Tap in: at the entrance gate, tap your T-money on the reader. Wait for the gate to unlock.
  6. Ride to your stop. If you transfer, usually you stay within the paid area—just follow the transfer signs for your next line.
  7. Exit: when you arrive, follow the signs to the exit gates and tap your T-money again to tap out, then go outside.
  8. Foreigners tip: Save a screenshot of your destination in the app (Korean + English). Use it to confirm the correct station on the platform board.
Bus
Seoul bus from scratch (step-by-step):
  1. Open an app like Naver Map or KakaoMap and search your destination to see the bus number and the correct stop name.
  2. Make sure you have a T-money card (used for paying bus fares in many cities, including Seoul).
  3. Go to the right stop and wait for your bus (check the bus number on the display).
  4. Board through the front door. Tap your T-money at the reader when you get on.
  5. As you approach your stop, press the stop bell/request button at the right time.
  6. Get off, and tap your T-money again at the exit reader (many buses have a tap-out near the rear door area).
  7. If you transfer, follow the app and tap correctly each time you ride.
  8. Foreigners tip: Show (or save) the stop name from the app in your phone and compare it to the digital stop screens so you do not miss the stop.
KTX (High Speed Train)
KTX from scratch (step-by-step):
  1. Buy your ticket (T-money is NOT for KTX). You can book online or buy at the station.
  2. Check your booking for your train name, departure time, and especially your seat number / car number.
  3. Arrive early (about 20–30 minutes is a safe buffer), then follow station signs to the correct KTX platform area.
  4. Use the station screens / staff help to confirm the platform and where your car will stop.
  5. When boarding, find your car and seat. If you have a mobile ticket, use it at the entry/check point as instructed.
  6. Board the train and sit in your reserved seat. No tapping is needed during the ride.
  7. At arrival, follow signs to the exit and your transfer route (if you have one).
  8. Foreigners tip: Keep your ticket QR/screenshot and seat number visible offline, and look for the car-number markings on the platform (they are key when signage is busy).
